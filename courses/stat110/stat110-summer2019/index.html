Midterm exam

The midterm exam will be available in D2L beginning Tuesday, May 28 and must be complete by Monday, June 3 by 11:59 pm. The exam covers Lectures 1-10. All corresponding practice quizzes are also available through 6/3 for you to practice. You must attempt the Week 3 graded quiz in order to access the exam, and you must take the multiple choice portion before you are allowed to take the short-answer portion. You are allowed 50 minutes on each portion. A practice exam and key are available below. Of course, you must attempt both portions alone: no collaboration of any kind is allowed. You may use your notes, as long as they are written on paper (your laptop will be locked down) and a calculator on the exam.

Final exam

As stated last week, I will be proctoring the final with a video conferencing room. In order to take the exam, all students will have to be in the video conference room (I will send a link later this week). You will need to turn on your web cam, show your ID (so I know it’s really you taking the exam!), and do a quick scan around the room with your web cam so I know you are alone. If you do not have a web cam you will need to purchase one (your WSU laptop should have one built-in). I have provided 6 time slots and you can sign up for the one you want to take the final: Wednesday, Thursday, Friday from 9-11 AM or 1-3PM. If none of these work with your schedule please let me know.

To sign up for a time slot in D2L:

  • Go to Communication > Groups
  • Enroll in the time slot during which you want to take the final exam.

The format will be similar to the midterm. The final exam will consist of a multiple choice portion (60 minutes; must be completed first) and a written portion (60 minutes; must be completed second). Both portions are open-note. Practice exam and key are available on the course website. The exam will be roughly half cumulative and half material covered since the midterm.
